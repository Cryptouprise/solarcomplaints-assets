<h2>What a Sunrun Buyout Actually Is</h2>

<p>A Sunrun lease or PPA is a 20 or 25-year contract where Sunrun owns the panels on your roof and you pay them monthly. A "buyout" is an option in your contract that lets you purchase the system from Sunrun early — at a price Sunrun calculates.</p>

<p>There are generally two triggers for wanting a buyout:</p>

<ol>
<li><strong>You want to sell your home</strong> and cannot or do not want to transfer the lease to the buyer</li>
<li><strong>You want to stop paying Sunrun</strong> and own the equipment outright</li>
</ol>

<p>Either way, the buyout price quoted by Sunrun is the key variable. And the quoted price is where the math gets interesting.</p>

<h2>The Three Components of the Sunrun Buyout Formula</h2>

<h3>Component #1: Present Value of Remaining Payments</h3>

<p>This is the biggest chunk. Sunrun calculates the total of your remaining monthly payments across the life of the contract — with the 2.9% annual escalator applied — and discounts them to present value using an interest rate Sunrun chooses.</p>

<p>Here is why this is problematic:</p>

<ul>
<li>Your remaining payments are <strong>already inflated</strong> by the escalator. Year 10's payment is already $194, not $150.</li>
<li>The "present value" discount rate Sunrun uses is typically lower than market rates, which <em>inflates</em> the present value rather than deflating it.</li>
<li>This component alone often accounts for 80-90% of the buyout quote.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Component #2: "Fair Market Value" of the Equipment</h3>

<p>Some contracts require Sunrun to quote fair market value as the buyout. In theory, this should approximate the resale value of used equipment. In practice, Sunrun's FMV calculations consistently come in at 2-4x realistic market rates.</p>

<p>A 10-year-old panel array has a real market value of maybe $4,000-$8,000. Sunrun's FMV calculation for the same array often runs $20,000-$30,000. The difference is built into the formula methodology that heavily weights replacement cost over resale value.</p>

<h3>Component #3: Administrative and Transaction Fees</h3>

<p>Buyouts trigger various administrative fees — "termination fees," "processing fees," "title transfer fees." These can add $500-$3,000 to the quoted buyout.</p>

## FAQ Schema

### How is the Sunrun buyout price calculated?

The Sunrun buyout formula has three components: (1) the present value of all remaining monthly payments under the contract, with the 2.9% annual escalator already factored in, discounted using a rate Sunrun chooses; (2) a 'fair market value' calculation of the equipment that consistently runs 2-4x realistic used market prices; and (3) administrative, termination, and transfer fees. The largest component is typically the present-value calculation of remaining payments, which alone can account for 80-90% of the quoted buyout.

### Why is my Sunrun buyout quote so high?

The buyout is designed to be unfavorable. Sunrun's business model depends on 25-year revenue streams that are bundled and sold to capital markets. Every buyout represents lost future revenue Sunrun is attempting to protect. The formula therefore prices buyouts above realistic equipment value — typically 2-4x the used market price — to discourage exits. A 10-year-old system worth roughly $6,000-$10,000 used frequently results in a $30,000-$40,000 Sunrun buyout quote.

### Do I have to pay the Sunrun buyout to get out of my lease?

Often, no. Four legal paths regularly produce contract cancellation with equipment retention and zero buyout payment: sales misrepresentation claims under state consumer protection law, forged or irregular signature claims that void the contract, improperly disclosed 3-day cooling-off periods that may still be open, and leverage from active Attorney General investigations. Before paying any buyout quote, a free case review is worth the 20 minutes to understand which path may apply to your contract.

### Can I sell my house without paying the Sunrun buyout?

Yes, in many cases. Sunrun leases can be transferred to the buyer if the buyer credit-qualifies with Sunrun and is willing to assume the obligation. Many title companies will approve clear title with the lease recorded as a disclosed encumbrance rather than requiring the buyout. 'You must buy out to sell' is often pressure rather than a legal requirement. If the buyer refuses to assume, legal cancellation of the lease is a cleaner option than paying a buyout.

### Is the Sunrun buyout quote negotiable?

The official position is no. In practice, Sunrun will sometimes negotiate down when legal pressure is applied — but not through the customer service channel, which is trained to hold the line. Negotiation happens through attorneys, formal legal demands, or settlement discussions in the context of an active cancellation claim. If you have grounds for cancellation (misrepresentation, forgery, cooling-off, AG leverage), that becomes negotiation leverage that customer service will not offer on its own.
