### Did Freedom Forever go out of business?

Not exactly. Freedom Forever filed Chapter 11 bankruptcy on April 15, 2026, which is a reorganization filing — not a liquidation. They have publicly stated they intend to keep operations running including their 25-year production guarantee. Based on the Sunnova and SunPower bankruptcies in 2024, expect successor entities, warranty disputes, monitoring fees, and deteriorating customer service as the case progresses. The filing lists 50,000-100,000 creditors and court documents state no funds will be available for unsecured creditors after administrative expenses.

### Do I still have to pay my solar loan if Freedom Forever went bankrupt?

Your solar loan is a separate contract with a separate lender — usually Mosaic, GoodLeap, Sunlight Financial, or Dividend. Legally, that loan continues despite Freedom Forever's bankruptcy. However, you have strong grounds to challenge or cancel the loan under the FTC Holder Rule, which says lenders are subject to the same claims and defenses you have against the seller. When your installer collapses and cannot deliver the warranty, production guarantee, or service that justified the loan, that becomes the basis for loan cancellation or major reduction. Do not stop paying on your own — get a legal strategy first.

### What happens to my Freedom Forever warranty?

The warranty is at serious risk. Freedom Forever says they intend to honor their 25-year production guarantee, but courts can void warranty obligations in reorganization, and successor entities routinely refuse to assume them. The same thing happened with SunStrong Management after the Sunnova and SunPower bankruptcies — SunStrong explicitly told customers 'We did not assume Sunnova Production Guarantee or Limited Warranty obligations.' Expect the same playbook. Document your system performance, save all your warranty paperwork, and file a proof of claim in the bankruptcy to preserve your rights.

### What is the Freedom Forever bankruptcy case number?

Case number 26-10522, filed April 15, 2026 in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the District of Delaware. The company is represented by Morris, Nichols, Arsht and Tunnell LLP. Liabilities are reported between $500 million and $1 billion, with assets between $100 million and $500 million. The largest unsecured creditor is Mosaic Funding IX at $114 million.

### Can I cancel my Freedom Forever contract because of the bankruptcy?

Not automatically — but the bankruptcy creates strong grounds for cancellation that did not exist before. When an installer cannot deliver the warranty, production guarantee, or service that was the basis of the contract, state consumer protection laws and the FTC Holder Rule open the door to either full contract cancellation (with equipment retention) or major loan reduction, typically 40-60%.
