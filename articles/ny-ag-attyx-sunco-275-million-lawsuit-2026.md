<h2>The Four Allegations That Make This Case Different</h2>

<h3>Allegation #1: Forged E-Signatures</h3>

<p>Most solar misrepresentation cases are about <em>what was said</em> versus <em>what was signed</em>. This one goes further. The complaint alleges Attyx sales staff <strong>"simply forged consumers' e-signatures on the agreements"</strong> in some cases. Legal Aid Society attorneys investigating independently reached the same conclusion: one client's e-signature "appeared on documents she did not recognize."</p>

<p>Forged signatures are not a consumer protection gray area. They are <strong>contract void ab initio</strong> — meaning the contract never legally existed in the first place. You cannot be bound by a contract you did not sign. If your e-signature was forged on any Attyx or SUNco document, the contract is likely unenforceable from day one. (<a href="/solar-contract-forged-signature">Full guide to forged signatures</a>.)</p>

<h3>Allegation #2: Documents Never Given to Consumers</h3>

<p>The complaint alleges that Attyx <strong>"repeatedly obtained consumers' electronic signatures… without first providing the consumer with copies of the agreements they were signing."</strong> Tablets were used specifically to prevent review. <h3>Allegation #3: Hidden Loan Surcharges</h3>

<p>Attyx's lending partners, Solar Mosaic and WebBank, allegedly <strong>embedded substantial loan surcharges inside the stated "system costs"</strong> rather than disclosing them as finance charges. This is a Truth in Lending Act (TILA) violation in addition to a New York GBL §349 violation.</p>
