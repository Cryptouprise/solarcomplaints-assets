<h2>Why Forged Signatures Are the Strongest Possible Claim</h2>

<p>Most solar cancellation cases involve <strong>misrepresentation</strong> — the sales rep said one thing, the contract said another, and you have to prove the gap. That works, but it is a fight.</p>

<p>A forged-signature claim is categorically different. If your signature was forged:</p>

<ul>
<li>The contract is <strong>void ab initio</strong> — treated as if it never existed</li>
<li>There is no contract to misrepresent around</li>
<li>There is no contract to enforce</li>
<li>There is no valid lender relationship, because the lender's loan is secured by an invalid agreement</li>
<li>Full restitution and contract cancellation are the standard remedies</li>
</ul>

<p>You are not arguing about whether the salesman said your bill would be zero. You are arguing that <em>you never signed the thing</em>. That is a categorically stronger legal position, and courts and settlement negotiators treat it as such.</p>

<h2>The Four Signatures Patterns That Signal Forgery</h2>

<h3>Pattern #1: Signatures That Do Not Match Your Actual Signature</h3>

<p>The most obvious. Compare the e-signature on your contract to your actual signature on other documents — driver's license, bank signature card, tax returns, handwritten checks. Look for:</p>

<ul>
<li>Slant direction (right-leaning, vertical, left-leaning)</li>
<li>Letter spacing and proportions</li>
<li>Starting and ending stroke shapes</li>
<li>Whether your signature typically includes a middle initial, and whether the e-signature does</li>
<li>Pressure-mimicking artifacts (real signatures have natural variation in stroke weight; forgeries often do not)</li>
</ul>

<p>If the signature on your contract is cleaner, more uniform, or structurally different from your actual signature style — document it with side-by-side photos.</p>

<h3>Pattern #2: Inconsistent Signatures Across Contract Pages</h3>

<p>A multi-page contract signed by the same person at the same time should have consistent signatures across pages. When different pages show materially different signature characteristics — different slant, different letter formation, different spacing — it suggests someone else signed at least some of the pages.</p>

<p>The NY AG's Attyx complaint references exactly this pattern: reps "forged consumers' e-signatures on the agreements" page-by-page when they needed to.</p>

<h3>Pattern #3: Initials in Places You Do Not Remember Initialing</h3>

<p>Many solar contracts require initials on specific clauses — escalator disclosures, arbitration waivers, tax credit acknowledgments. If you find initials on clauses you do not remember initialing, or on pages you do not remember seeing at all, that is evidence of post-signing tampering.</p>

<h3>Pattern #4: Tablet Signing Artifacts</h3>

<p>Real tablet-captured signatures contain natural artifacts — slight tremors, variable pressure, natural curve variations. Forged tablet signatures (especially those generated by software or re-traced by a sales rep) often lack these artifacts. Some forensic examiners can detect the difference with high accuracy.</p>

## FAQ Schema

### What happens if my solar contract signature was forged?

A forged signature renders the contract void ab initio — meaning it is treated as if it never legally existed. This is categorically stronger than a misrepresentation claim. You are not arguing the contract was signed based on false statements; you are arguing there is no contract because you did not sign it. Under contract law, your solar loan tied to the void contract is also unenforceable, and under the FTC Holder Rule any claims against the installer flow to the lender. Full contract cancellation with restitution is the standard outcome.

### How do I prove my solar e-signature was forged?

Compare your contract signatures to known legitimate signatures from your driver's license, bank signature card, passport, or canceled checks. Look for differences in slant, letter spacing, stroke pressure, and whether you typically include a middle initial. Inconsistencies between signatures on different pages of the same contract are particularly strong evidence — a real person signing the same contract produces consistent signatures. Initials on clauses you do not remember initialing also support the claim. Save everything as photos or PDFs.

The AG is also investigating the lenders — Solar Mosaic and WebBank — alongside Attyx for funding these allegedly fraudulent contracts.

### Can I cancel my solar loan if the purchase contract was forged?

Yes. When the underlying purchase contract is void for forgery, the related solar loan loses its consideration — the thing you were supposedly buying. Under contract law this generally renders the loan unenforceable. Beyond that, the FTC Holder Rule makes the lender (Mosaic, GoodLeap, Sunlight, Dividend) subject to the same claims you have against the seller.
