<h2>The Five Legal Paths to Cancel a Sunrun Contract After Installation</h2>

<h3>Path #1: The 3-Day Cooling-Off Period — Wider Than You Think</h3>

<p>Federal Trade Commission rules and virtually every state give door-to-door sales customers a 3-business-day right to cancel. If Sunrun sold you the contract door-to-door (or through a home visit) and did not properly disclose this right in the exact manner the law requires — including a written notice on the specific required form — <strong>your cancellation window may still be open years later.</strong></p>

<p>The rule is called the "FTC Cooling-Off Rule" at the federal level and varies by state. What most homeowners do not know: the 3-day clock only starts when the required notice is given properly. Improperly given notice means the clock never started. (<a href="/solar-cooling-off-period-missed">Full cooling-off period guide</a>.)</p>

<p>If you cannot locate a proper notice of cancellation in your contract documents, your 3-day window may still be available.</p>

<h3>Path #2: Sales Misrepresentation Under State Consumer Protection Law</h3>

<p>This is the most common path. If the salesperson made specific promises that turned out to be false — "your bill will be zero," "you qualify for the tax credit," "you own the system," "no payments until it's on" — those promises are actionable under state Deceptive Trade Practices Acts even if they are not in the written contract.</p>

<p>State statutes supporting this include:</p>

<ul>
<li>Texas DTPA (triple damages + attorney's fees)</li>
<li>California CLRA and UCL</li>
<li>New York GBL §349</li>
<li>Florida FDUTPA</li>
<li>Connecticut UTPA</li>
</ul>

<p>The statute of limitations typically runs from the date you <em>discovered</em> the deception — not when you signed. If you just figured out the savings were a lie, your clock is starting now. (<a href="/solar-salesman-promised-zero-electric-bill">Zero bill misrepresentation guide</a>.)</p>

<p>If the e-signature on your Sunrun contract does not match your actual signature style, or if you signed in a way you do not remember, or if different documents have signatures that do not look consistent — the contract may be <strong>void ab initio</strong> (never legally existed). ## FAQ Schema

### Can I really cancel a Sunrun contract after the system is already installed?

Yes — but not through Sunrun customer service. There are five legal paths: the 3-day cooling-off rule (which may still be open if Sunrun did not disclose the right properly), sales misrepresentation claims under state consumer protection law, forged or irregular e-signatures, FTC Holder Rule claims against your solar lender, and leverage from active AG investigations. The best outcome is full contract cancellation with the equipment staying on your roof at no further cost.

### How much does it cost to get out of a Sunrun lease through a buyout?

Sunrun's buyout quotes typically run $25,000-$40,000 for equipment worth $8,000-$12,000 used — priced to discourage exit. The buyout is the most expensive way to leave a Sunrun contract. Legal cancellation paths often result in zero payment with equipment retained, or 40-60% reduction in the remaining contract value. Get a case review before paying any buyout quote.

### What is the FTC Cooling-Off Rule for Sunrun contracts?

The FTC Cooling-Off Rule and most state equivalents give door-to-door sales customers 3 business days to cancel a contract. For the clock to start, the company must provide specific written notice of the right to cancel in the required format. If Sunrun did not give you this notice, or gave it improperly, the cancellation window may still be open years later. This is one of the cleanest cancellation paths when it applies.

### Should I stop paying my Sunrun lease while I fight the contract?

No. Unilateral default destroys your credit and gives Sunrun (or the lender) a clean default lawsuit that makes your position much worse. The correct path is to pursue legal cancellation through state consumer protection laws, the FTC Holder Rule, or signature challenges — then stop paying only after the contract is legally canceled. A case review will tell you which path applies and the expected timeline.

### Does it matter if I signed my Sunrun contract on a tablet?

Potentially, yes. The NY AG lawsuit against Attyx specifically alleges that tablet-based e-signatures were used to bypass document review, and in some cases signatures were forged. Compare the e-signatures on every page of your Sunrun contract to your actual signature. Inconsistencies, signatures on pages you do not remember seeing, or signatures that do not match your usual style all support a forgery or contract-void claim. This is the strongest cancellation path when it applies.
